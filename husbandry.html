Home Feeding Handling Sources

Ball pythons like to hide, so the more cluttered the better

Things you need:
  • hide
  • heat lamp with a dimmer
  • terrarium (there's many options)
  • substrate
  • food of the appropriate size
  • water bowl (that the snake can't tip)
  • spray bottle or humidifiier

    • I've raised my female Ball Python for the last 3 years, and this some of my own experience, and from sources that I trust whole-heartedly.

    Once you get the handle on taking care of Ball python, and get them on a good routine, then it becomes a lot easier.

    Daily Morning Routine:
      1. turn on the heat lamp, and check temperatures
      2. check humidity and boost humidity by using humidifier or using a spray bottle
      3. I always will look at my snake, and do a "wellness check". (i.e Check to see if she's shedding, make sure she's hasn't escaped, etc. )
    Nightly Routine:
      1. check temperatures, and humidity
      2. do another wellness check
      3. shut off heat lamp (if it's cold in my room, then I'll use a Ceramic Heat Emitter. It puts out a lot of heat, so I only use it in Winter)

    The temperature range you should be keeping Basking surface — 95-105°F (35-40°C) Ambient/air — 82-86°F (28-30°C) Night drop — 72-78°F (22-26°C)

    Humidity should on average be 55% to 65%. The average humidity in Utah (where I live) is about 30%, and needs to be lifted. If your snake is shedding, and then the humidity needs to be higher.
